Security & compliance

How we protect your credentials

You're trusting DevBook with API keys and team data. Here's exactly how we handle them — no vague claims, just specifics.

🔐

AES-256-GCM encryption at rest

Every API key stored in your vault is encrypted with AES-256-GCM — the same standard used by AWS, banks, and government systems. Keys are decrypted only at the moment of use and never stored in plaintext.

🔒

TLS encryption in transit

All connections between your browser and DevBook use TLS 1.2+. Your requests, responses, and credentials are never transmitted unencrypted. We enforce HTTPS on all endpoints.

🚫

No payload logging

We never log your request bodies or API responses on our servers. Your data passes through to execute the request and is not stored, analyzed, or accessible to DevBook staff.

👥

Team access controls

When a team member leaves, remove them from your workspace. Their access to shared templates and the key vault is revoked immediately. You control who can see and use your credentials.

🚀

Team onboarding — how it actually works

Getting your team set up takes minutes, not meetings. Here's the process:

Step 1

You create a workspace and add your shared API keys to the vault. Only you control what keys are available.

Step 2
